About Beth

I am an incoming PhD student in Marketing at City University of Hong Kong. My academic background spans Economics and Professional Accountancy, followed by more than four years of experience in marketing communications and public relations. Working closely with brands and consumer-facing campaigns made me increasingly curious not only about what people choose, but why they choose it. I am now turning that practical experience into research questions about consumer behavior, pricing and promotion, AI and trust, and digital interfaces. This website is a space to share ideas as they develop and connect with others who find them interesting.

Research Interests

  • Consumer Behavior

    Understanding the psychological processes behind everyday judgments and choices.

  • Pricing and Promotion

    Exploring how discounts, gifts, timing, and risk-reduction policies shape purchase decisions.

  • AI and Consumer Trust

    Examining how AI disclosure and technology influence trust, authenticity, and engagement.

  • Digital Interfaces and Online Choice

    Studying how interface and product-presentation decisions affect evaluation and choice.
